Alan Williams

About

Alan is an Olivier and WhatsOnStage Award-winning Musical Supervisor and Director. He is also an Arranger, Orchestrator and Pianist who studied piano and conducting at Chetham's School of Music and the Guildhall School of Music and Drama.

His career has seen him work alongside some of the most renowned Theatre Composers and Directors - such as Rob Ashford, Bob Avian, Jack O'Brien, Andrew Lippa, Jamie Lloyd, Andrew Lloyd Webber, Michael Mayer, Alan Menken, Jerry Mitchell, Jason Moore, Casey Nicholaw, Marc Shaiman, Matthew Sklar, Stephen Sondheim, Susan Stroman, Jeanine Tesori, Michael Wierner & Alan Zachary.

Biography

Theatre credits include: Sunset Boulevard (St. James Theatre, New York & Savoy Theatre, London) - Olivier Award for Outstanding Musical Contribution, and the WhatsOnStage Award for Best Musical Direction/Supervision, Elf (Marquis Theatre, New York & Dominion Theatre, London), Crazy For You (Gillian Lynne Theatre & Chichester Festival Theatre), Funny Girl (August Wilson Theatre, New York, US tour, Menier Chocolate Factory, Savoy Theatre, UK Tour & Cinema broadcast), Hairspray (London Coliseum), The Boy in the Dress (Royal Shakespeare Company), Evita (Open Air Theatre, Regent's Park), The Sorcerer's Apprentice (Southwark Playhouse), Big Fish (The Other Palace), Disney's Aladdin (Prince Edward Theatre), A Damsel in Distress (Chichester Festival Theatre), Assassins (Menier Chocolate Factory), Urinetown (St James Theatre & Apollo Theatre), The Commitements (Palace Theatre & two UK tours), A Chorus Line (London Palladium), Shrek (Theatre Royal, Drury Lane, two UK tours & Australian production), The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee and Passion (Donmar Warehouse), Monty Python's Spamalot (Palace Theatre), Eric's (Liverpool Everyman) and Little Shop of Horrors (West Yorkshire Playhouse).

He produced the cast albums of Sunset Boulevard & The Boy in the Dress, Matt Croke - Only Dreaming, and conducted on the London revival recording of Funny Girl. He also played keyboards on the Original Cast Recording of Sister Act. His orchestration and arrangement of the Overture of Overtures for YouTube was performed as the opening to 50 years of Broadway (Kennedy Center, Washington).

Workshops include: 13 Going On 30, King Ralph, Catch Me If You Can, Sin, The Go-Between, Mr Vertigo and Billy Elliot.

TV appearances include: The Royal Variety performance, Olivier Awards and BBC Children in Need.
